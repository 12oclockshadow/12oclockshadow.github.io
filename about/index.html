All about us.

Hey there. We're 12 O'Clock Shadow. We play a fusion of Jazz, Blues, and swing, kicking it on the east side of Seattle.

Book us

  • Mike Pautz

    A native to the Seattle area, Mike rediscovered his passion for making music after a 20+ year hiatus and has not looked back since. Mike plays tenor sax for 12 O'clock Shadow (but also plays alto and bari saxophone additionally). He is responsible for co-founding the band and is the acting co-manager.

  • Jeff Miller

    Jeff likes to let the music speak for itself.

  • Robert Mobbs

    Rob grew up in Gulfport MS and came to the Seattle area in the mid-90’s.He pays his bills as a software engineer and spends whatever money is left buying drums. In addition to drumming for 12 O’Clock Shadow you can catch him laying down the rhythm for the local established classic rock cover band The Pin Drops.

  • Daniel Bruck

    Dan is a lifelong musician with a background that includes the U.S. Army Band, Berklee College of Music, the University of Washington graduate school of music, and numerous ensembles of all types and sizes. He thinks playing in 12OS is a blast! In his spare time Dan is an acoustical designer and President of BRC Acoustics & Audiovisual Design, an acoustical consulting firm based in Seattle. He lives in Issaquah with his piano-playing wife, Lee, and sons Miles & Alex.

  • Dan Welter

    Originally from Houston Texas, Dan has played Guitar, Bass and Piano since his teenage years. Prior to his recent move to the Northwest he played with various Classic Rock, Jazz and Big Bands in the Houston area. Now when not performing with the 12 O'clock Shadows, he does manufacturing engineering for Microsoft and is co-founder and business manager for a family-owned medical clinic.

  • James Borthen

    A conspiring co-founder of the band, Mr. Swing is an engineer by day, and a jammin’ hipster by night. He balances parenting, girl friends, and a music career on a knife edge while he explores the Seattle music scene. He just missed being named Kenmore Jazz Musician of the Year, but, with continuing fortitude, continues to hone his skills searching for those perfect musical arrangements that make 12 O’Clock Shadow a study in variety, swing, and eclectic styles.

  • Cameron Davison

    Cameron Davison is shrouded in mystery. Rumor says he was either born in Bellevue, or at a mental hospital in Hell. His childhood was fraught with danger on the hard, hard streets of suburban Issaquah, where he fell into bad company as a numbers runner for the Scottish Mafia. His early forays into organized crime were cut short when he took a bullet to the knee, and he then focused his efforts into playing the Alto Sax . Now he spends his time directing the Pacific Cascade Big Band, playing in the 12 O'Clock Shadows and moonlighting in the evenings as a crime fighting superhero on the hard, hard streets of suburban North Seattle.

  • Justin Beckwith

    Justin is the gregarious purple trombone toting musician found lurking about the 12 O'Clock Shadow's shows. In his spare time, he keeps busy writing software and teaching his kids how to get into trouble. He's still looking to find a way to incorporate his tuba and ukulele skills into a show.

  • Bob Jones

    It had been quite a while since Bob sang in high school or studied voice in college, but a few years ago, Jim Borthen (bari saxophone), convinced Bob that singing jazz and the blues should be his next big adventure. Bob took his advice, continues to hone his vocal skills, and looks forward to exploring the edgier side of the American Songbook with 12 O’Clock Shadow.

  • Dave Pollon

    Dave’s an expat Canuck, living in Seattle long enough to have learned a thousand words for rain. Software lured him to West, but that doesn’t seem to matter anymore compared to the challenges of being a youth sports Dad! Dave channeled Hendrix in the 70s, Gilmour in the 80s and Navarro in the 90s but now that he’s all grown up he doesn’t sound like any of those guys. He’s super excited to being a part of 12 O’Clock Shadow and bringing soulful swing and blues sounds to the people. He can also be found around Seattle with his other band, TrebleMaker.
